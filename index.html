My personal experience with formal education environments has been a bit rocky, and creating a primary school that focuses on fostering individuality and equality between students was, and will continue to be, of utmost importance to me. By integrating elements of Montessori pedagogy and Multiple Intelligence theory into the design of the school from the start, the project ensures an environment that caters to the individual needs of each and every student, rather than a typical one-size-fits-all curriculum.

PROJECT TIMELINE: FEBRUARY 2024 - MAY 2024
MODEL TIMELINE: 5 DAYS, MAY 2024
The classroom clusters best illustrate these principles; students who are more socially inclined can joyfully communicate between classrooms. The space created by lifting the clusters off the ground provides a covered outdoor learning area for students with a naturalistic inclination. Visuo- spatial students have a field day exploring the elevational changes within the classroom. Body/kinesthetic students can engage tactically with material and textural variances. Essentially, the classroom is designed with every intelligence type in mind, ensuring that it becomes a place where diversity is celebrated, potential is recognized, and weaknesses are cared for.

DRAWING(s) TIMELINE: MARCH 2024 - MAY 2024
PLANS, ISOMETRIC & SECTION: MARCH - APRIL 2024
MASTERPLAN - MAY 2024
The most public feature of the school sits on the northeast corner of the site, inviting parents to participate in the school’s philosophy. The top floor consists of small pods and a desk that faces the school field and classroom clusters, acting as a shared workspace for the parents. This creates a place for parents with children who may need extra assistance to be nearby. Beneath the shared workspace is a public forum, or multipurpose hall, giving the newly developed Green Square a civic centre. The ground floor houses the school’s assembly hall. The building is constructed almost entirely from glue-laminated timber and CLT, with the only exceptions being joinery and the concrete base.

DRAWING TIMELINE: MAY 2024
Student accommodations have the potential to create an intellectually and socially stimulating environment. However, they can also become isolating, expensive, and overly focused on efficiency, reflecting developers’ priorities and profit optimization rather than addressing the individual and collective needs of students. A strategy to enhance the student experience is to incorporate qualities derived from an established restorative program, such as a stepwell. Originally designed for harvesting, collecting, and storing water in arid regions of India, stepwells have evolved into architectural celebrations of community.

Images display individual contributions to a group project with fellow students Joshua Dambiec & Kathryn Du Pre le Roux. Model was a group effort.

PROJECT TIMELINE: AUGUST 2023 - NOV 2023
MODEL TIMELINE: 1 WEEK, NOV 2023
While a source of water is the most essential component of a stepwell in its most primitive form, they’ve now become more socially oriented. Part of this shift was the way they’ve been articulated. By creating a labyrinth of staircases, it allowed the user to curate their own experience. To seek sun or shade, to find the best spot for a cool breeze - it empowers them. The concept of utilizing steps as a means of organizing and empowering social spaces holds particular relevance for students. Implementing such a repetitive organizational scheme promises to introduce variation in movement, foster dynamic social engagement, and instill an explorative spirit among the student community.

DIAGRAMS, ISOMETRIC TIMELINE: OCTOBER 2023 - NOV 2023
While the external central staircase offers a sprawling landscape of social opportunities, the main study and creative spaces, kitchens, and laundries are situated within an open plane enclosed by a timber slat system. This design provides a tranquil retreat from the dynamic energy of the steps. Incisions through the slats reveal the only social moments within those spaces, at times tracing the contour of the staircase as a symbolic representation of their social power.

ELEVATIONS TIMELINE: NOV 2023
The “House Project” brief aimed to disrupt the conventional features of a typical suburban family home through a series of radical incisions. The goal was to uncover possibilities within the transformed space to accommodate the anticipated turnover of occupants. I approached these interventions as a way to encourage creative behavior, fostering a connection with nature within the home, enabling seamless bleed between domestic programs, and crafting versatile spaces capable of adapting to future changes.

PROJECT TIMELINE: AUGUST 2022 - NOV 2022
MODEL TIMELINE: 2 DAYS, NOV 2022
The design process began with an exhaustive cycle of iteration, involving numerous tracings and handheld models, ultimately leading to the selection of the final design. The implemented incisions, although highly intrusive, created a unique sequence of movement that enabled nature to penetrate deep into the house, fostering a conducive environment for the occupants’ creative pursuits. The final plans showcase a modular and irregular composition, ensuring the adaptability of the house for future generations of creators.
